about

The Strong Leaders of the Future organization is a non-profit organization based in Los Angeles, California. The organization is dedicated to helping youth and local community members become thriving leaders.

It's mission is to empower the next generation of leaders across Los Angeles by fostering practical leadership skills, civic engagement, and collaborative problem-solving within our diverse and vibrant communities. Through grassroots mentorship, accessible workshops, and project-based initiatives, we equip residents with the tools to drive positive change and build a more resilient and equitable city from the ground up.

history

SLF was founded in 2005 by former community organizer Elena Rodriguez, who sought to empower residents of Los Angeles with practical leadership skills to address local challenges.

In 2005, Elena Rodriguez, disheartened by a perceived lack of local empowerment in her community, envisioned an organization that would equip residents with the skills and confidence to lead their own change initiatives.

SLF began with a series of small, accessible workshops held in local community centers, covering topics like public speaking, community organizing, and project management.
Rodriguez and her early volunteers prioritized reaching residents from underserved communities, using a grassroots approach to build trust and demonstrate the value of the training.

It eventually grew to become the strong and successful organization it is today. Rodriguez's efforts paved the way for new leaders to start their own organizations. Her efforts lead to the creation of the triumphant annual SOAR event.

volunteer

Volunteers are a crucial aspect of SLF. Despite our success, SLF consistently struggles with securing stable and sufficient funding; often relying on grants and volunteer efforts.

This limits our ability to scale our programs and reach more communities. Yet, as community leaders and volunteers work togther to aid SLF with funding and daily operations, their continued support makes the impossible possible.

The organization has to navigate complex and often competing interests within Los Angeles's diverse political landscape, sometimes finding it difficult to remain neutral while still serving a broad base of community members.

SLF needs volunteers to advocate and cement its reputation as a key incubator for local leadership. Volunteers help develop engagement with citizens in local communities and raise funds for daily operations. They are crucial to continue to shape the future of Los Angeles.
